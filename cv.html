// Graduate research

Bao Lab, Rice University (2020 – present). Advisor: Gang Bao, Ph.D. Integrating experimental and computational methods to decode gene-editing outcomes in human disease models.

Project

Fine-mapping IBD eQTL regulatory variants

Built eCROP-seq, a base-editing functional screen across immune cell contexts; screened >4,000 common variants from IBD GWAS credible sets and validated >400 causal variants; extended to rare-variant screening and ABE/CBE single-nucleotide editing.

Project

Long-read decoding of large gene modifications

Designed LV_caller for detecting large unintended deletions, insertions, and inversions from CRISPR/Cas9 editing via SMRT-seq; quantified large deletions (11.7–35.4%) at therapeutic loci (HBB, HBG, BCL11A, PD-1) in HSPCs and T cells.

Project

Single-cell genotype-to-transcriptome platform

Developed GoT-EDIT, integrating CRISPR editing outcomes with 10x transcriptomic readouts; applied across erythroid differentiation stages in SCD patient HSPCs and to base editing in primary T cells.

Project

Machine learning for off-target prediction

Benchmarked deep-learning models for Cas9 off-target prediction; evaluated transfer-learning strategies for cross-dataset generalizability. Also developed GISA-seq for genome-wide AAV integration-site analysis.
