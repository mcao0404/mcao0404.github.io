// Research

My doctoral work in the Bao Lab integrates wet-lab and computational methods to decode CRISPR editing outcomes across human disease models — from fine-mapping causal disease variants to characterizing the unintended structural changes that determine therapeutic safety. Three major threads are listed below.

Regulatory variant function

Developed a CRISPR/Cas9 functional screen (eCROP-seq) that fine-maps candidate causal IBD eQTL variants across immune cells at single-cell resolution — revealing that regulatory associations often reflect multiple causal variants on a shared haplotype.

Editing safety & fidelity

Developed LV_caller that uses long-read sequencing (SMRT-seq) to detect and quantify large unintended gene modifications, paired with deep-learning benchmarks for off-target prediction — a rigorous picture of editing precision at therapeutic loci.

Genotype-to-transcriptome

Developed scGoT-EDIT, a single-cell platform that connects highly diverse CRISPR editing outcomes directly to transcriptomic phenotypes in disease-relevant cell models, linking genotype to cellular fate.

Functional dissection of IBD variants reveals haplotype-embedded regulatory variation

A central achievement of my doctoral work is a large-scale functional genomics study integrating CRISPR/Cas9 nuclease and base editing with bulk- and single-cell RNA-seq to fine-map causal regulatory variants in Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD). We developed a moderate-throughput workflow of the eCROP-seq platform, pairing lentiviral perturbation with single-cell transcriptomic readouts, and applied it to interrogate over 4,000 IBD-associated variant candidates across 89 genes, identifying and validating more than 400 causal variants across multiple immune cell types. By comprehensively linking genetic perturbations directly to transcriptional consequences at single-cell resolution, we demonstrate that regulatory associations often reflect multiple causal variants on a shared haplotype, not the single variant typically assumed. The work is a co-first-authored manuscript currently under revision at Science.

Characterizing unintended on-target editing outcomes

My second major focus is the systematic characterization of unintended on-target editing outcomes — large deletions, insertions, and complex rearrangements invisible to conventional short-read sequencing (NGS). I built LV_caller, a pipeline leveraging PacBio SMRT sequencing to sensitively and quantitatively profile large deletions and large insertions at editing sites. Applied to edited Hematopoietic Stem and Progenitor Cells (HSPCs) from Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) patients, it revealed that large deletions occur at high frequencies, challenging the prevailing assumption that such events were rare (Science Advances, 2022) and shifting how the field evaluates therapeutic editing safety. I extended the method to a Sickle-HUDEP2 reporter model (Mol Ther Nucleic Acids, 2026), in vivo base editing of LPA in transgenic mice (Molecular Therapy, 2026), and a comparative study of Cas orthologs (Cell Biomaterials, 2026), and co-authored a review on CRISPR-induced modifications and their clinical implications (Curr Opin Biomed Eng, 2023).

Off-target prediction & integration-site safety

Complementing the on-target work, I conducted systematic evaluations of deep-learning models for off-target prediction, assessing generalizability across independent validated datasets and identifying key limitations in cross-dataset performance (Small Methods, 2025), building on earlier transfer-learning analyses (bioRxiv, 2021). I also developed GISA-seq for detecting genome-wide AAV-donor integration events, applying it to demonstrate the safety of the novel Repair Drive method (Sci Transl Med, 2025).

Deep-learning models · AAV integration

Linking genotype to phenotype at single-cell resolution

I developed the scGoT-EDIT pipeline (single-cell Genotyping of Transcriptome for EDITing outcomes), which integrates CRISPR genotyping with transcriptomic profiling to link diverse editing outcomes directly to cellular phenotypes. It enables systematic mapping of genotype–phenotype relationships across heterogeneous editing outcomes at single-cell resolution, offering new insight into how editing influences cellular fitness, differentiation and selection. The manuscript has been submitted and under review.

Other Collaborations

Beyond my individual research contributions, I have mentored four undergraduate and one master's students in gene editing and computational biology, and have provided bioinformatics consultation to the Genetic Design and Engineering Center (GETC) at Rice and Baylor/Rice Genome Editing Testing Center (GETC). These efforts have extended the impact of my work beyond my own research projects and contributed to multiple successful collaborative studies.

// Tools

Named methods and pipelines I developed, several across multiple papers.

  • eCROP-seq Moderate-throughput expression-CRISPR droplet screen for functional fine-mapping of disease-associated regulatory variants. Wet-lab · Functional Screen · eQTL · Single-cell RNA-seq
  • LV_caller Long-read pipeline for detecting and quantifying large unintended gene modifications (deletions, insertions, inversions) from CRISPR/Cas9 editing. Computational · SMRT-seq · Large Gene Modifications
  • GISA-seq Anchored-PCR NGS assay with UMI-tagged adapters for unbiased genome-wide retrieval of AAV-donor integration sites. Computational · NGS · AAV Integration Sites
  • scGoT-EDIT Single-cell genotyping-to-transcriptome platform integrating CRISPR editing outcomes with 10x transcriptomic readouts. Computational and Wet-lab · Single-cell Genotyping and RNA-seq
