// Research

My doctoral work in the Bao Lab integrates wet-lab and computational methods to study genome editing outcomes across human disease models. Three threads run through it.

Regulatory variant function

A base-editing functional screen (eCROP-seq) that fine-maps causal IBD eQTL variants across immune cell contexts, linking genotype to expression at single-cell resolution.

Editing safety & fidelity

Long-read sequencing to detect large unintended gene modifications, plus deep-learning benchmarks for off-target prediction — building a rigorous picture of editing precision at therapeutic loci.

Genotype-to-transcriptome

GoT-EDIT, a single-cell platform that connects CRISPR editing outcomes directly to transcriptomic phenotypes in disease-relevant cell models, including SCD patient HSPCs.

// Research statement

My doctoral research integrates experimental and computational methods to decode CRISPR editing outcomes — from fine-mapping causal disease variants to characterizing the unintended structural changes that determine therapeutic safety. Four threads, and the tools that came out of them, define the work.

Read full statement Collapse statement

Functional dissection of disease-associated variants

A central achievement of my doctoral work is a large-scale functional genomics study integrating CRISPR/Cas9 nuclease and base editing with single-cell RNA-seq to fine-map causal regulatory variants in IBD. I developed the eCROP-seq platform — a moderate-throughput workflow pairing lentiviral perturbation with single-cell transcriptomic readouts — and applied it to interrogate over 4,000 IBD-associated variant candidates across 89 genes, identifying and validating more than 400 causal variants across multiple immune cell types. By linking genetic perturbations directly to transcriptional consequences at single-cell resolution, this is one of the most comprehensive functional dissections of complex-disease variants to date. The work is a co-first-authored manuscript currently under revision at Science.

Characterizing unintended on-target editing outcomes

My second major focus is the systematic characterization of unintended on-target editing outcomes — large deletions, insertions, and complex rearrangements invisible to conventional short-read sequencing. I built LV_caller, a pipeline leveraging PacBio SMRT sequencing to sensitively and quantitatively profile structural variants at editing sites. Applied to edited HSPCs from SCD patients, it revealed that large deletions occur at high frequencies, challenging the prevailing assumption that such events are rare (Science Advances, 2022) and shifting how the field evaluates therapeutic editing safety. I extended the method to a Sickle-HUDEP2 reporter model (Mol Ther Nucleic Acids, 2026), in vivo base editing of LPA in transgenic mice (Molecular Therapy, 2026), and a comparative study of Cas orthologs (under revision, Cell Biomaterials, 2026), and co-authored a review on CRISPR-induced modifications and their clinical implications (Curr Opin Biomed Eng, 2023).

Linking genotype to phenotype at single-cell resolution

I developed the scGoT-EDIT pipeline (single-cell Genotyping of Transcriptome for EDITing outcomes), which integrates CRISPR genotyping with transcriptomic profiling to link editing outcomes directly to cellular phenotypes. It enables systematic mapping of genotype–phenotype relationships across heterogeneous editing outcomes at single-cell resolution, offering new insight into how editing influences cellular fitness and differentiation. A manuscript is being finalized for submission.

Off-target prediction & integration-site safety

Complementing the on-target work, I conducted systematic evaluations of deep-learning models for off-target prediction, assessing generalizability across independent validated datasets and identifying key limitations in cross-dataset performance (Small Methods, 2025), building on earlier transfer-learning analyses (bioRxiv, 2021). I also developed GISA-seq for detecting genome-wide AAV-donor integration events, applying it to demonstrate the safety of the novel Repair Drive method (Sci Transl Med, 2025).

Mentoring & collaboration

Beyond my own projects, I have mentored four undergraduate and one master's student in gene editing and computational biology, and provided bioinformatics consultation to the Genetic Design and Engineering Center at Rice and the Baylor/Rice Genome Editing Testing Center — extending the impact of my work into multiple successful collaborative studies.

// Tools

Named methods and pipelines I developed, several across multiple papers.

  • LV_caller Long-read pipeline for detecting and quantifying large unintended gene modifications (deletions, insertions, inversions) from CRISPR/Cas9 editing. SMRT-seq · structural variants
  • GoT-EDIT Single-cell genotyping-to-transcriptome platform integrating CRISPR editing outcomes with 10x transcriptomic readouts. single-cell · base editing
  • eCROP-seq Moderate-throughput expression-CRISPR droplet screen for functional fine-mapping of disease-associated regulatory variants. functional screen · eQTL
  • GISA-seq Anchored-PCR NGS assay with UMI-tagged adapters for unbiased genome-wide retrieval of AAV-donor integration sites. integration sites · safety
